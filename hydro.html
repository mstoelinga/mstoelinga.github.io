NWSvc Northwest River Forecast Center (NWRFC)

Main page

  • Main page shows map with river gauge sites. Gauge sites are colored according to maximum predicted river category over the next 10 days
  • Can always return to this page by going “home” (on some pages), or to “River and Hydrology” menu, “10-day forecast points”.
  • Click on individual sites on map to get key recent observations and 10-day forecasts. Parameters shown depend on site. Each site clicked produces a non-disappearing tab on right side, allowing for easy access to a number of sites.
  • When on the main page, right-side menu offers useful controls.
    • River and Hydrology (the default)
    • Observed Precipitation
      • Shows recent precipitation estimates at sites on map. Note these do not agree with measurements, they include other factors considered by NWRFC, like undercatchment, radar-aided estimation, etc. Can select 6-hourly amounts (with ability to navigate in time), or last 24 h.
      • Mouse over a site to see a meteogram of precip at the site.
      • There is a weird locking of the refresh on this display…occasionally have to completely leave and return to site to refresh.
    • Forecast Precipitation
      • Click on 240, 24, or 6 hour.
      • Map will populate with site-predicted total precip amounts.
      • Mouse over a site to see a meteogram of forecasted precip at the site.

10-Day Temperature and Precip Forecast

  • Shows day 1-10 forecast maps of the NWRFC domain, with precip, Tmax, Tmin, and Snow Level
  • Forecast Trend tab is a very useful way to evaluate the past forecasts and D(prog)/Dt
  • Can also get here from main NWRFC page, Weather Forecasts menu, 10-Day Temp and Precip
  • A few important things on this page:
    • On left: measurement tool is helpful.
    • Layers menu
      • USGS stations: Turn on streamflow, surface water levels, and precipitation
        • Streamflow/gauge sites will be circles, precip sites will be diamonds

USGS National Water Dashboard (Top-Level Water Data Page)

  • A few important things on this page:
    • On left: measurement tool is helpful.
    • Layers menu
      • USGS stations: Turn on streamflow, surface water levels, and precipitation
        • Streamflow/gauge sites will be circles, precip sites will be diamonds
      • Hydrology: turn on watershed boundaries. “Rivers” helps as well, but they are exactly redundant with the river labels in the “Topographic” basemap, so not needed.
      • Base map: I like “Topographic” best
    • Legend: has useful legend-type information on what you’ve displayed
  • Click on a site to see graphs. A graph page will open, but go directly to the “Monitoring location” page for this site by clicking on the “Site page” button at the top of the just-opened graph page. The “Monitoring location” page has more stuff.
    • Scroll down to row of large rectangular buttons for some useful options:
      • View related graphs gives three important options:
        • Gives a link for the starting point for a combined location graph…a powerful and helpful tool. Can put the same parameter from multiple sites on the same graph.
          • Example for Green River: https://waterdata.usgs.gov/combined-location-graph/#parameterCode=00060&period=P7D&locationId=USGS-12105900&locationId=USGS-12106700&locationId=USGS-12113000
        • All continuous graphs: Plots all available parameters as separate graphs at once, stacked vertically, instead of toggling between them
        • Daily data graphs: self-explanatory
      • Download data, View data records: self-explanatory
